Web Version of Adalogical Ænigma #29
Take each Neighbor on a walk starting at their Numbered House

Rules

Preamble: Each numbered square represents a house occupied by one neighbor about to take a walk, where the attached brown rectangle is that house’s front porch.
  1. The resident takes a walk, only along grid lines, exiting their house by the porch and making exactly as many right-angle turns as their house number.
  2. Finally the neighbor enters some house (possibly their own).
  3. No two neighbors’ paths meet or cross outside of a house.
  4. All of the houses are linked together by paths into a single connected neighborhood.
